Paleo's PEDESTRIAN CROSSING: A Lesson in Timefighting
March 27, 2009 - 10:09pm narrative otis.pig

(album review)

We here at Timefighters are living a lie. We''ve known it all along, however, only an ocean of drink could possess us to admit this in the company of others. We wake up every morning blinded by the futility of our struggle. It''s not the sun blaring through our curtainless windows that blinds us, it is Time. We can fight Time all we want, but Time will always win. No matter what weapons we wield, no matter what inventions we mother. In the same vein as umbrellas fighting rain, or the Golden Gate Bridge fighting California''s sink into the ocean, Time will always win. That is the lesson we can garner from Paleo''s new (and old) album, Pedestrian Crossing (Little Egypt Records).

water for all: preserving olympia's artesian heritage
March 27, 2009 - 10:14pm narrative sabalu

Along Fourth Avenue and Jefferson Street, characters of all kinds find their way to the greatest treasure a parking lot can offer: Olympia''s most beloved artesian well. The very best water in Thurston County just might spring from that old pipe between spots forty-three and forty-four. Its neck peaks out from cement blocks, with water flowing outward into an almost heart-shaped pool before heading toward a nearby storm drain. A mural with stenciled images of well-goers watches over the humble pipe, reminding visitors of the collective responsibility urged in protecting a shared source of water.
