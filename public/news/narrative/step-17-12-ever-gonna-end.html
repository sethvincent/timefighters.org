Step 17 1/2: Is This Ever Gonna End?

  • View
  • What links here

You want to know something funny? I talked an 8 ½ month old baby girl out of a tizzy-fit, with her strong-armed in my left hand and a spatula off-handed in my right. Yeah, I cooked a midnight snack with Luka Jane in my arms; waggle your fingers in front of my face about my hot kitchen, moms. We got to watch; sausages sizzle, eggs fry, bread get soft in the toast-en over, feel the crisp window air on our face, and me say, “I got your back” to the baby-sitter. It was one of those classic: I got home, knew someone else in the house would want to eat, and loudly heard the baby in her room. After sixty seconds of manic crying, and me secretly wondering if the babysitter had thrown the baby and found a rope, I heard a soft sound. Cat. No that’s silly. Yuri G for “Gonna Get You You Little 4 Legged Bitch” left the house three months ago. Then I remembered the mattress in Luka Jane’s room, and it all clicked together. My god I thought, the babysitter’s been in there (for upwards of 30 minutes) shushing the baby, AND I’M HEARING HER OVER THE CRYING ON THE BABY 1-WAY RADIO. Check out all the things I did while she was with the baby AND a roommate was in the garage:

-Opened the door loudly, making three dogs (the fourth lives in the garage) bark like I was Five-Oh.

-Shushed them all, and moved them outside to find a urinal and two stalls.

-Did a thorough check of the kitchen and living room, making sure no humors had exited the dogs.

-Put a bunch of clean laundry on a hamper-mountain of other clean laundry.

-Moved my wet clothes from a hot-heavy wash mode into the dryer, flipped to a heavy-hot dry mode.

-Did the dishes.

-Cracked an egg in the pan, sliced a kielbasa long ways, and tossed four cut halves into the skillet.

-Realized how I was going to cook the next breakfast round as I forgot about that piece of toast.

After I walked it out with Luka Jane (breakfast happily frying in the kitchen), I put her into her highchair. She got locked in (like we knew what we were doing), and I proceeded to play the game: What Can the Baby Eat? Rice cake <yummy!>, I thought, as I broke one apart and placed it in front of her. She proceeded to find some leftover greens stuck to her chair, and happily put them back in her mouth. Good I thought, as I bit into my saved breakfast round; an egg cooked real thin wrapping up kielbasa, on a cream cheese <Gromit!> piece of toast. “Thank you so much,” cooed the babysitter, “I had no idea you had food cooking, I thought the faucet was running!” Boom, in comes Ms. Koftinow, red cheeks and no bulging pockets. She proceeds to tell us how she gracefully bowed out of the on-going Wednesday night poker game, assuming after a few unanswered text messages she was probably needed back on the home front. Her Spidey-Sense for Moms drew her to the ultimate rescue, and the war was won. “The funniest thing about this so far though,” I thought to myself, “IS THAT NO ONE KNOWS I CHANGED A FLAT TIRE AFTER I LEFT POKER!” You guessed it kids, I was totally at that Wednesday night poker game, and totally remembered how to change a tire, which you can now learn in, “These Easy Steps”.

-Step one: Oh shit, question why the car is pulling to the right like it knows something you don’t.                                                                -Step two: Probably because you asked Matt to move his truck, and you still ran over the side of the driveway when you left.               -Step three: Pull off into a wet-ass ditch, and realize your parked flat tire is pointing uphill IN THE DITCH.
-Step four: Decide driving < ½ mile is ok on one flat-ass tire, and make it to an Electricity Power Tower.
-Step five: Park your whip with the flat tire facing the work floodlight shining into the parking lot.
-Step six: Get the spare out of your trunk, and all the metal, too (jack, tire iron, and rod).
-Step seven: Loosen the tire’s nuts with a tire-iron left kick (no joke), until they’re all wiggly by hand.
-Step eight: Snag the jack; twist the end knob to raise its jaw on THE METAL FLAP behind the front wheel.
-Step nine: Grab the rod, slip it through the end knob, and two-hand twist that tire into the air.
-Step ten: Keep your head ducked as that car beams by, take off the nuts AND the tire.
-Step eleven: Put on your spare tire, with the air nubbin for the tire facing OUTWARDS.
-Step twelve: Hand-spin your nuts on the spare, TIGHTENING EVENLY BY ALTERNATING NUTS EACH TURN.
-Step thirteen: Hand-turn the tire out of the air with the rod and listen for tire to SLOW-GRIND GRAVEL.
-Step fourteen: EVENLY tighten the nuts using the tire iron, KICKING those nuts tight until the car rolls.
-Step fifteen: Put your flat tire (you rolled it, it hissed), back in the trunk, along with your hardware.
-Step sixteen: Drive away clean (dirty hands), realizing at step five you called a car buddy, didn’t answer. 
-Step seventeen: Decide you were just seeing if they wanted to kick it at 11:30pm, no big deal.
-Step eighteen: PULL THAT MILKY WAY OUT OF YOUR GLOVE BOX, YOU EARNED IT BUD!
