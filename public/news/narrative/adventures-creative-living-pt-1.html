Adventures in Creative Living, Pt 1

  • View
  • What links here

It's 1:00 am on Sunday, December 28, and I am flipping through a recent impulse-buy, a book called Dwelling Portably. It''s a compilation of newsletters about portable dwelling and long camping. I'm looking to see if they have any alternatives to refrigeraters. I haven't found any yet. I'm trying to decide if I could do this. My alarm is set for 8:00 am and in the morning (the real morning), when I will put my left and right feet into two pairs of socks and big rain boots and muck my way downtown to the transit center, where I will wait for the bus.The bus will take me to look at look at a 12x12 cabin with loft. It has no utillities, only a wood-stove for heat and solar powered light. Which might be a little extreme right now, in the middle of winter. But if not now, then when? 

The scenario presents more constraint than I have experienced so far, but it has garden space to offer and the cost of utilities would non-existent or rather, equal to the price of wood.  The rent is slightly less than I currently pay for a creatively furnished open loft area. If I can learn to live without the conveniences of running water, central heating and in-home internet, it would cut down on rent a little and utillities completely as I'm pretty sure there is a way to get wood.The ad for the cabin, perhaps ironically found on CraigsList while searching for a new room-mate for my current house, has somehow triggered a latent protestant-work ethic and conservative guilt into meeting up with liberal reactionary ideals about global warming, limited non-renewable energy use, social justice and the search for a (the good) consistent life. Who knew? In the past I have lived in a damp, hand-built hexagonal hippy cabin without running water, as well as a house that had been stripped for renovation, with only cold running water. All told, I lived without a shower for about a year. It wasn't bad and I managed school and jobs in both. Both of these places did however have limited electricity that could power a space heater and a hotplate.

The real challenge that a cabin like the one I'm going to look at tomorrow would pose is food preparation. Ever since working for a summer on an Organic Farm, my diet has been irrevocably changed to consist mostly of local farm products. Winter produce like beets and potatoes require a relatively long cook time and I imagine spending several hours over a single meal and then not having a place to store leftovers. Hmmm. Though I haven't found anything about food storage yet in the mish-mash of chronological and (apparently) typewritten newsletters that makes up Dwelling Portably, I do see several other tidbits that look fun. My current room-mates are tolerating my food scraps bucket and re-usable container box, but they aren't in favor of either compost or firepits in the back yard.  The little 12x12 cabin would not be a culmination of anything, but could maybe provide a sort-of a test run for learning to live creatively.  I haven't even seen the cabin yet! Of course the place could be a total shack. Or a Rainbow Child might beat me to it. But failing that, would I be willing or able to make a go of it? It''s now about 3:00 am and that could well be taking it's toll on my thought process. Maybe I'm completely insane and the rising sun will bring me to my senses.

What do you think?
