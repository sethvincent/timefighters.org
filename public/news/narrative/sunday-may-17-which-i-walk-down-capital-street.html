Sunday, May 17 - In Which I Walk Down Capital Street

  • View
  • What links here

After several rainy days, Sunday the 17th of May brings blue skies and an unusual warm breeze, and I step out of the house blinking in wonder. Buses run from 18th ave to downtown every 15 minutes, but I prefer to walk through the capital campus. This morning a male in an orange reflective vest sits on a cooler on the opposite side of Capital.

I take the crosswalk and head straight downtown, grinning an bemused. Orange cones mark off a whole lane on the left side of the road. A shirtless elderly man jogs passes in front of me along the coned off lane. He holds his elbows high and his hands dangle in front of his chest. Sleek seal-like women carry their shapes smoothly, and pass groups of walkers. Lean, solo-runners appear made of bone and tendon, bottles strapped across the backs of their polyester uniforms. A limper with a taped knee and a few every day people looking miserable in soaked cotton t-shirts and clingy shorts straggle throughout.

Each intersection holds a cheerful crossing guard. At capital and 14th, a police officer stands with legs spread and barks “You can go now,” at each runner and pedestrian. The pedestrian traffic is thickening and each group includes at least one dog. Runny-eyed puppies, labs and bulldogs are all leashed on their best behavior.

Three young girls hold a homemade banner, while one yells “Run Mommy, Run!” while the other two shriek. As I approach, they break free from the sidelines to run alongside a tired and determined jogger. The young girl runs backwards in front of her, yelling “Come on Mom, you can do it!”

A husky adolescent voice bellows “Go Dad!” A male in his early thirties, outfitted in crisp outdoor gear leans out from the crowded sidewalk and searches those descending the hill, camera half-lifted.

The finish line hangs at the base of Capital hill, where it meets up with Fifth and Sylvester Park and a large time clock runs passes 3:07:00. Orange mesh stretches from below the banner, out across the intersection. There is a large banner reading Capital ½ Marathon. Curious. I stop to do the calculations on my phone. A half marathon is about 13 miles, so the runners passing me were averaging 4 1/3 miles per hour, or just over a 13 minute mile. That makes sense, I know it takes me 15 minutes to walk the mile to downtown.

Later that night I look online and find the results already posted. Those crossing the finish line were 800-809 of 878 total participants. The elderly man who passed me that morning was Ronald Book of “Puyalip[sic],” WA, one of two participants over the age of seventy.

All of downtown was enjoying the weather; a few blocks away the fountain was overflowing with young children in bathing suites and sun dresses having their own festivities. After a dark, secluded winter and a tempestuous spring, the sultry weather and conviviality is a welcome and long awaited change.
